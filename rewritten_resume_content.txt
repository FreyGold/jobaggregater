LinkedIn profile improvement guidance (from notes.txt transcript)

Headline
- Remove vague qualifiers like “Aspiring” and replace with a clear target role + specialty.
- Add specific keywords you want to be found for (e.g., Angular, React, Node, iOS/Android, AWS/Azure, Data Engineering, etc.).
- If you’re broad (Full Stack), still name your primary stack (e.g., “Full Stack Developer (React + Node)” vs “Full Stack Developer”).

Profile photo + banner
- Use a centered, well-lit headshot where your face is clearly visible.
- Prefer a friendly/approachable photo (smiling helps).
- Avoid dark, moody, or overly “artsy” crops that make you harder to recognize.
- If you use a banner, use it to help people contact you or quickly understand your brand (website/email/what you do). Keep it clean.

About section
- Keep it short and readable: aim for a 15-second scan.
- Write in “common speech,” not as a keyword dump.
- Include: what you do now, what roles you’re targeting, and your primary tools/keywords.
- If you’re listing skills, write them in sentences (e.g., “In my current role I work with…”) instead of a raw comma list.

Featured section (high-value real estate)
- Add portfolios, project demos, case studies, or posts showcasing your best work.
- If you include a resume PDF, consider putting it elsewhere and using Featured for proof of work (especially for front-end/UI/mobile).
- For front-end/UI/UX/mobile roles: Featured should be “littered” with examples (screenshots, live links, short walkthroughs).

Experience section
- Add 3–5 bullets for each role that explain what you actually did.
- Write so a non-technical recruiter can understand it.
- Use plain-language outcomes and impact (e.g., “reduced turnaround time,” “increased throughput,” “improved reliability”).
- Include measurable results where truthful (%, time saved, volume handled, growth). If you don’t have numbers, add scope (team size, users, frequency, scale).
- If your title is vague, clarify in the first bullet what the job really was (what you were responsible for).
- If you’re freelance/consulting: list projects/clients/outcomes; avoid looking like “self-employed” with no proof.
- If you’re an intern/junior: document everything you did; be more granular than you think you need to be.

Recommendations
- Get at least 1 recommendation; more is better.
- Strong recommendations can be a major trust signal when recruiters/hiring managers don’t know you.
- Ask internship leads, managers, mentors, or clients to write one.

Skills + endorsements
- Recruiters may skim this mainly to gauge your focus.
- Endorsements can help as a lightweight signal (e.g., 100+ endorsements on a skill suggests credibility).

Licenses/certifications
- If a cert is truly valuable (especially cloud certs), surface it where it’s easy to see (About/headline-style positioning).
- Avoid over-valuing minor course certificates; include them only if they help round out your profile.

Interests
- Follow companies and topics relevant to what you want.
- Following the company you’re applying to can be a small, easy win.

Content posting (to get discovered)
- Post proof of work and progress: project launches, lessons learned, small wins, before/after outcomes.
- Don’t wait for engagement; posting still gives recruiters/hiring managers “something to hook onto.”
- Consider posting a short “mock interview” video answering common first-round questions (tell me about yourself, strengths, conflict story, what you’re working on).
- If you have unfinished projects: document your build process (short recordings/walkthroughs) so people can see how you think.

Networking vs. applying
- Don’t rely only on applications.
- Use LinkedIn to message people at the company (developers, team members) and ask who the hiring manager is.
- Start conversations using something specific from their profile (shared school, volunteering, interests) instead of “hey.”
- If rejected, stay professional—being a strong “second choice” can pay off later.

Quick checklist
- Clear keyword-rich headline
- Clean centered headshot
- About section readable in 15 seconds
- Featured section shows proof (projects/portfolio)
- Experience bullets written for non-technical readers + measurable impact
- At least 1 recommendation
- Relevant interests followed
- Consistent posting cadence (even small updates)
